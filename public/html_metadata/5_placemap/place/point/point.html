point

noun

UK /pɔɪnt/ US /pɔɪnt/

1. an idea, opinion, or piece of information that is said or written

 

2. an opinion or fact that deserves to be considered seriously, or that other people agree is true

 

3. a particular time or stage reached in a process

 

4. a particular place

 

5. purpose or usefulness

 

6. a mark or unit for counting, especially how much a person or team has scored in a sport

 

7. a unit used for measuring the size of printed letters, equal to about 0.3 mm or 1/72 of an inch

 

8. the sharp end of something, such as a knife

 

9. a particular quality or characteristic of a person or thing

 

10. a long, thin area of land that stretches out into the sea

 

11. a small, round spot that is used in numbers to separate whole numbers from parts of numbers:

 

12. a socket to which a wire from a piece of electrical equipment is connected in order to supply it with electricity or a radio, television, or other signal

 

13. in some car engines, either of two parts that allow or prevent the flow of electricity

 

14. a small, round mark on a line, plan, or map to show the position of something

 

15. a mark on a compass that shows direction, such as north, south, east, and west

 

16. a very small, round light that you can see in the distance

 

 

point

verb

UK /pɔɪnt/ US /pɔɪnt/

1. to direct other people's attention to something by holding out your finger towards it

 

2. to hold something out in the direction of someone or something

 

3. If something points in a particular direction, it is turned towards that direction
