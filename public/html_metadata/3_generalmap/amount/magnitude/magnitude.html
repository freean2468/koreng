pad

noun [ C ]

UK /pæd/ US /pæd/

1. a piece of soft, thick cloth or rubber, used to protect a part of the body, give shape to something, or clean something

 

2. a number of pieces of paper that have been fastened together along one side, used for writing or drawing on

 

3. a hard flat area of ground where helicopters can take off and land, or from which rockets are sent

 

4. one of the large, flat leaves of a water lily

 

5. the soft part under the paw (= foot) of a cat, dog, etc.

 

6. a person's house or apartment

 

 

pad

verb

UK /pæd/ US /pæd/

1. to put pieces of soft material in something to make it soft, give it a different shape, or protect what is inside

 

2. to walk without making a noise
