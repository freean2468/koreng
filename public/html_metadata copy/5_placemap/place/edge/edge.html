edge

noun

UK /edʒ/ US /edʒ/

1. the outer or furthest point of something

 

2. the side of a blade that cuts, or any sharp part of an object that could cut

 

3. the point just before something very different and noticeable happens

 

4. an advantage over other people

 

5. a small but noticeable amount of anger in someone's voice

 

 

edge

verb [ I or T ]

UK /edʒ/ US /edʒ/

1. to move slowly with gradual movements or in gradual stages, or to make someone or something move in this way
